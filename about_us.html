About Us

My Company is created by Khmer investor.



Vision

We see a world where Cambodian handicrafts are increasingly sought after and cherished by locals and tourists because of their exceptional quality and deeply embodied value.


Mission

Our mission is to preserve Cambodia’s traditional artistry through exceptional craftsmanship and the creation of high-quality handmade products, enhancing lives and preserving cultural heritage for future generations.


Purpose

To build on our 25-year legacy by making high-quality products that appeal to both local and international audiences, guided by the values of craftsmanship, quality, authenticity, and preservation.


Values

AUTHENTIC:Our dedication to preserving Cambodia’s traditional artistry ensures that every item we produce is authentic, embodying the rich cultural heritage of Khmer arts and crafts.

HANDCRAFTED:Every product at Artisans Angkor is meticulously made by hand, in honor of the artisanal skills passed down through generations.

Cambodian Culture Context

With a rich tradition of handicrafts ingrained in the Khmer culture, a workshop with a traditional skill specific to the area can be found in every village and every province of Cambodia. Weaving in Takeo, silversmith in Kandal, marble in Pursat and ceramic in Kampong Chhnang.

As per figures from Statista.com, the market for accessories in Cambodia is estimated to rake in a total revenue of $8 million in 2024. The annual growth rate for the market has been projected at around 4.94 percent as per the compound annual growth rate (CAGR) from 2024 to 2028. Breaking down the market further, the luggage and bags segment accounts for the greatest market volume to the tune of $0.45 billion this year.

Cambodia is a country of ancestral talents and know-how, said Pierre-Andre Romano, Chairman and founder of Satcha Handicraft Incubation Center. For more than a thousand years craftsmen and women have demonstrated amazing skills that one can still admire not only on the antique sites of the Khmer empire but also in every province and even villages.

There are numerous ways the handicraft industry can provide job opportunities for the youth, said Monika Nowaczyk, CKO, Cambodia Knits. In addition to the craftsmanship, design and production of items, the sector requires talent, skill and leadership in branding, marketing and sales. There are numerous opportunities for enterprising youth to support the growth of the handicraft sector while positively contributing to the economy and preserving Cambodia’s cultural heritage.

Cambodia boasts a rich tradition of handicrafts, brimming with potential to empower local communities and safeguard cultural heritage, felt Catherine Germier-Hamel, CEO and Founder of Millennium Destinations. Promoting these crafts isn’t just desirable, it’s essential.

Globally, it is China in the leadership position in this category with revenue totalling $116 billion in 2024. In terms of individual revenue generation in the accessories market in the Kingdom, revenue per person, taking into account the size of the population, is approximately $46.86 this year. Revenue from online sales alone is estimated to fetch 17.9 percent of the total revenue in the market for accessories in 2024. There has been an increasing demand for traditional Cambodian handicraft accessories among tourists.

The handicraft industry is one of the main pillars of Khmer identity and a selling point for the destination as a touristic intangible landmark, that’s why it must be preserved, continued Romano.

Unfortunately, the masters are getting old, and some skills may be lost if they are not transmitted to the younger generations. It is time for them to train the youth and offer them the opportunity to find a job based on an expertise they will be proud to practice.

In addition to the craftsmanship, design and production of items, the sector requires talent, skill and leadership in branding, marketing and sales, said Nowaczyk. There are numerous opportunities for enterprising youth to support the growth of the handicraft sector while positively contributing to the economy and preserving Cambodia’s cultural heritage.

The future of Cambodia’s handicraft industry is bright. By strategically investing in skill development, market access and sustainability, this sector can become a significant driver of fair and harmonious economic growth. It can transform Cambodia into a creative hub, recognised globally for its craftsmanship while safeguarding its unique cultural identity.
